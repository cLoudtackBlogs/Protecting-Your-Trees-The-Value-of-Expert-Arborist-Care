What Is an Arborist and Why Should You Hire One?

An arborist is a trained professional specializing in cultivating, managing, and maintaining trees and shrubs. Certified arborists undergo extensive training to understand tree biology, diseases, and proper care techniques.

Our expertise ensures that trees remain healthy, safe, and aesthetically pleasing, which is essential for both residential and commercial properties.

Benefits of Hiring a Certified Arborist

Tree Health Expertise: Arborists can diagnose and treat diseases, nutrient deficiencies, and pest infestations. Their knowledge allows them to recommend effective treatments, helping trees recover and thrive.

  • Safety and Risk Mitigation: Trees with overgrown or damaged branches can pose significant safety risks. Arborists are trained to recognize and address potential hazards, reducing the likelihood of falling branches or tree-related accidents.
  • Correct Pruning Techniques: Improper pruning can harm a tree, weakening its structure and making it susceptible to disease. Arborists use industry-approved techniques that promote healthy growth and maintain the tree’s structural integrity.
  • Tree Preservation: While some trees may require removal, arborists prioritize preservation whenever possible. They assess the tree’s overall health and recommend treatments to prolong its life, only removing it when absolutely necessary.

Signs You Need Arborist Services

Recognizing when your trees require professional attention is essential for maintaining their health and preventing property damage. Here are some common signs that indicate it is time to call an arborist:

  • Overgrown or misshapen branches: Trees with excessive or uneven growth may need pruning to maintain their shape and prevent safety risks.
  • Leaning trees: A tree that begins to lean unexpectedly may have weakened roots or structural issues, requiring immediate evaluation.
  • Visible decay or fungus: Fungal growth, such as mushrooms at the base, or visible decay could indicate internal rot.
  • Discolored or falling leaves: Leaves that are wilting, discolored, or falling prematurely may indicate disease or nutrient deficiencies.
  • Cracked or damaged bark: Cracks or peeling bark can signal underlying health issues or pest infestations.
  • Root damage: Exposed or damaged roots may lead to instability and poor nutrient absorption.

By addressing these warning signs early, you can protect the health of your trees and reduce the risk of costly damage.

Tips for Maintaining Healthy Trees Between Arborist Visits

While professional arborist services are essential, there are steps you can take to keep your trees in good condition between scheduled visits:

  • Watering: Water deeply and infrequently to encourage profound root growth. Avoid shallow, frequent watering, which can lead to weak root systems.
  • Mulching Apply a layer of mulch around the base of trees to retain moisture, regulate soil temperature, and suppress weed growth.
  • Regular Inspections: Periodically check your trees for signs of disease, pest activity, or physical damage.
  • Avoid Over-Pruning: Excessive pruning can stress trees and hinder growth. Leave major pruning tasks to certified arborists.
